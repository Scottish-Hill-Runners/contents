### Course change — Dollar Hill Race

Dollar winning times show a clear course change around 1997/1998:
- **1983–1997**: winning times ~38–43 minutes (e.g. 0:38:50, 0:42:54, 0:40:50)
- **1998 onwards**: winning times ~1:17–1:22 (e.g. `0:79:35` = 1:19:35, `1:22:27`, `1:17:23`, `1:21:26`)
- **2004** (48:05) is an anomaly within the post-1997 era — possibly a shortened/wet weather route

### Large entry count spikes (confirmed genuine)

These appeared as statistical outliers but investigation confirmed they are real large fields — winning times were consistent with other years and position numbers were sequential (no evidence of merged datasets):

| Race | Year | Entries | Notes |
|------|------|---------|-------|
| Eildons | 2002 | 240 | Gender-split positions (M and F both start at 1) — normal for this race format |
| Screel | 1991 | 281 | Unusually large field; consistent winning time |
| Criffel | 2002 | 258 | Unusually large field; consistent winning time |
| Screel | 2004 | 113 | Larger than typical; consistent winning time |
| Chapelgill | 2003 | 104 | Large field with faster winning time (18:50 vs 20–24 min other years) — possible route variation |

### Notes on further uncertain mappings

- **`races/BenLomondFunRun` (1997, 1998, 2000)**: Mapped from archive "Ben Lomond Junior Race". BenLomondFunRun is itself a short (1.5 distance, 80m climb) open/fun event, so these junior results are appropriate here.
- **`races/DevilsBeeftub` (1996–2000)**: Mapped from archive "Moffat Beeftub". Winning times and entry counts are consistent across all years including 2001–2005, supporting that this is the same race.
- **`races/TwoMamores` (2001–2002)**: Mapped from archive "Two Buachailles". Entry counts (32 and 23) are low but winning times (1:38:37 and 1:35:57) are consistent. Two Buachailles and Two Mamores are different races — **these should be moved to a new `TwoBuachailles` folder**.
