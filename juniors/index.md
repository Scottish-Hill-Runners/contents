# Juniors

## Introduction

Welcome to the Juniors section of the Scottish Hill Runners website where you can find information on different issues of interest to juniors either already involved in hill running or looking to find out more. If you are a junior and have a general question on hill running in Scotland, please contact Scottish Hill Runners.

## What Is Hill Running?

Hill running is basically running in hilly terrain, often in the more beautiful parts of the countryside. It is a fun sport, gets you fit and takes you to some great places. Most races for juniors are simply to the top of a hill and back down, usually by the same route. They almost always follow marked routes, but it is still a good idea to familiarise yourself with the route beforehand and take along a map of the route as well. There are hill races throughout the year, but the main season runs from May to September.

## What Kit Do You Need?

For most races the same running kit you would have for a cross country race will be fine. Your shoes should have good grip, and it may be worthwhile investing in some specialist studded hill running shoes. You should also bring along a waterproof top and bottoms, a compass and a whistle, plus something to carry them in, ideally a bum bag, in case the race organiser asks you to carry some or all of these, though normally you will not have to.

## Junior Age Limits

Many shorter races are suitable for juniors or have separate courses for juniors. It is important to note that, for the benefit of juniors, and especially to protect their growing bodies from undue stress, there is a UK Athletics rule that specifies the maximum length of course that juniors are permitted to race:

- Under 12: 3 km
- Under 14: 5 km
- Under 16: 8 km
- Under 18: 10 km

Age groups are defined by year of birth for juniors. For example, in 2026 athletes born in 2012 will race in the U16 category all year.

In addition, there may be courses where race organisers set more restrictive age limits, for example where there is a lot of climb or rough terrain. These age limits are stated in the calendar where known, but you are always advised to check with the race organiser before travelling. Fun runs are suitable for all ages, though usually any under 8s must be accompanied by an adult.

## Race Categories

Hill races are divided into categories to help you judge the difficulty involved.

### Climb and Terrain Categories

- Category A: should average not less than 50 m of climb per km of distance and should not have more than 20% of race distance on road.
- Category B: should average not less than 25 m of climb per km of distance and should not have more than 30% of race distance on road.
- Category C: should average not less than 20 m of climb per km of distance and should not have more than 40% of race distance on road.

### Race Length Categories

- Category S: short race, 10 km or under in length. Juniors can run most of these.
- Category M: medium race, over 10 km but less than 20 km. Juniors cannot run these unless they are over 18 on the day of the race and the race is insured by SHR.
- Category L: long race.

## Junior Competitions

There is a busy programme of races for juniors including the Junior Hill Running League which, in 2026, is a series of 7 hill races of which at least 3 must be completed. There is also a one-off National Championship Race organised by Scottish Athletics with medals for the top 3 male and female athletes in U14, U16, U18 and U20 categories. There are also international opportunities to represent Scotland and GB and NI. Keep up to date via the junior hill running Facebook page.

## Scottish Athletics Junior Hill Running League

For 2026, the league has categories of U18, U16, U14 and U12. U12 do not score but can claim a memento if they finish the series.

The 7 races in the 2026 Junior League are:

- 19 Apr: Dumyat, near Stirling
- 26 Apr: Lomonds Junior Races, Falkland
- 16 May: Broughton Heights (IYC U18 Trial Race), near Biggar
- 6 Jun: Braemar Junior Highland Games, Braemar
- 20 Jun: Eildon Junior Race, Melrose
- 16 Aug: Cademuir Rollercoaster (JHI Trial Race), Peebles
- 19 Sept: Suidhe Hill Race, Kincraig

The page notes that ten bonus points are awarded in the Broughton Heights and Suidhe races.

Please check the details of each individual event to see which race to compete in on the day, as there may be multiple senior and junior races.

From 2026 the junior league categories are:

- U18: born 2009-2010
- U16: born 2011-2012
- U14: born 2013-2014

Points are awarded to the first 18 individuals in each category:

- 1st: 25 points
- 2nd: 20 points
- 3rd: 17 points
- 4th: 15 points
- 5th: 14 points
- Then reducing by 1 point per place down to 18th: 1 point

The final league score comprises the athlete's best 3 race scores, plus 2 points for each race completed. If overall points are tied after the final league race, head-to-head record is used to determine the winner of the age category. Thereafter, points scored at the Scottish Athletics national championships at Broughton are used.

## Membership Of Scottish Athletics

scottishathletics is the governing body for hill racing held under their UK Athletics code in Scotland. You do not have to be a member of scottishathletics, or any other organisation, to compete in hill races, but you need to be a member to participate in events such as the Junior Championships and Junior League.
